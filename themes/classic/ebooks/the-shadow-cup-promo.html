About the Story

When Luka Morozov—a once-promising Croatian striker shattered by the loss of his wife and daughter—is recruited by a mysterious man named Kael to play for the Republic of Meridian, a nation that doesn't exist on any map, he enters the Shadow Cup: a tournament played in a stadium woven from mist and memory, between nations that were erased, displaced, or simply dreamed too hard.

There, he finds a team of the disappeared—a Brazilian midfielder who faked his own death, a Syrian goalkeeper repaying debts to the dead, a Nigerian winger erased for speaking truth. And a twelve-year-old girl named Ana, who draws the future before it happens.

Nine chapters. Eleven original illustrations. A story about grief, belief, and the impossible idea that hoping hard enough can make something real.
