Alexander Pope: The Rape of the Lock as a Mock Epic

This paper examines Alexander Pope's The Rape of the Lock as a mock-epic poem, analyzing how Pope uses the conventions of classical epic to satirize the trivial concerns of eighteenth-century aristocratic society. The poem's mock-heroic style elevates insignificant events to epic proportions, creating humor while also offering social criticism.
