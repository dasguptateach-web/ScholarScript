## Postmodernism in Literature: A Comprehensive Study

Introduction
Postmodernism represents a radical break from modernist traditions in literature. This paper examines the key features of postmodernist fiction, including metafiction, pastiche, fragmentation, and parodic intertextuality.

Key Theorists
Jacques Derrida's deconstruction challenges the stability of meaning in texts. Michel Foucault's analysis of power and discourse reveals how knowledge is constructed. Jean Baudrillard's concept of hyperreality describes the simulation of reality in contemporary culture.

Literary Techniques
Postmodern authors employ techniques such as metafiction, temporal distortion, intertextuality, magical realism, and historiographic metafiction.

Major Works
Thomas Pynchon's The Crying of Lot 49 exemplifies postmodern paranoia. Italo Calvino's If on a winter's night a traveler plays with narrative convention.

Conclusion
Postmodernism continues to influence contemporary literature by questioning grand narratives and embracing multiplicity.
