﻿## Elizabethan Poetry and the Sonnet Tradition The Elizabethan age witnessed an extraordinary flourishing of poetic forms, with the sonnet emerging as the dominant lyric mode. Sir Philip Sidney's Astrophil and Stella (1591) initiated a sonnet craze that would see Shakespeare, Spenser, and Daniel produce some of the finest sonnets in the English language. The English or Shakespearean sonnet, with its three quatrains and a concluding couplet, offered poets a flexible structure for exploring themes of love, time, mortality, and art. Unlike the Petrarchan sonnet's octave-sestet division, the English sonnet's final couplet provided an epigrammatic turn that became Shakespeare's signature.
