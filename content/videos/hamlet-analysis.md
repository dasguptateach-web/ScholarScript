# Hamlet's Soliloquies: A Line-by-Line Analysis

A detailed breakdown of all seven of Hamlet's soliloquies, examining:

1. "O, that this too, too solid flesh would melt" (I.ii)
2. "O, what a rogue and peasant slave am I!" (II.ii)
3. "To be, or not to be" (III.i)
4. "Speak the speech, I pray you" (III.ii)
5. "Tis now the very witching time of night" (III.ii)
6. "Now might I do it pat" (III.ii)
7. "How all occasions do inform against me" (IV.iv)

Each soliloquy is analysed for its rhetorical structure, thematic content, and dramatic function.
