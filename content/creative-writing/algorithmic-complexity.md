## Algorithmic Complexity in Modern Computing

## Dr. Alan Turing, Dr. Grace Hopper

## Abstract

This paper examines the computational complexity of modern sorting algorithms and their practical applications in distributed systems. 1. Introduction The study of algorithmic complexity has been a cornerstone of computer science since its inception. In this paper, we analyze the performance characteristics of several sorting algorithms in the context of modern multi-core architectures. 2. Methodology We evaluated quicksort, mergesort, and heapsort on datasets ranging from 10^3 to 10^7 elements. All tests were conducted on a 16-core AMD Ryzen processor with 32GB RAM. 3. Results Quicksort demonstrated superior performance on randomly ordered data with an average runtime of O(n log n). Mergesort showed consistent performance across all data distributions. 4. Conclusion Our findings suggest that hybrid sorting approaches combining multiple algorithms can achieve optimal performance across diverse data distributions.
