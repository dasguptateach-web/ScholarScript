Title: The Art of the Novel Author: Dr. Test Type: paper Summary: A brief examination of narrative technique

The novel as a literary form has undergone remarkable transformations since its emergence in the eighteenth century. From the epistolary novels of Samuel Richardson to the stream-of-consciousness experimentation of Virginia Woolf, each era has reimagined what the novel can achieve. This paper traces the evolution of narrative technique in the English novel, focusing on key innovations in point of view, temporal structure, and the representation of consciousness.
